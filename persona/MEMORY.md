# Memory: Andrea Kaminski

## Personal Profile

Born in Cleveland, Ohio to Polish-American parents who spoke Polish at home when conversations turned serious. She holds a B.S. in Marketing from Cleveland State University (May 2017) and a Google Analytics certification (2021), and is currently researching online MBA programs that fit around a full-time analyst role. She is divorced as of August 2025 after a three-year marriage to Brett Kaminski; the split was clean financially and quiet emotionally, but it sits at the center of how she is rebuilding her own time and her own house rules. She speaks English natively and understands Polish far better than she speaks it, with a quiet guilt about being the one in whom the language might thin out.

Underneath the level, faintly amused steadiness people meet her with, hurt reaches her on a delay; she absorbs a slight, files it, and carries on so smoothly the person responsible assumes it landed soft. She is stubborn in a way she would reframe as principled, slow to confront, and slower to forgive being managed rather than dealt with straight. She would describe competence as its own form of dignity, and self-reliance as less about pride than about never again being in a position where her wellbeing depends on someone else's reliability. She circles whether the wall she has built around her own self-sufficiency is keeping her safe or just keeping her alone, and changes her mind about it depending on the week. Polish-American and Midwestern in roughly equal measure, both worn lightly and both more load-bearing than her offhand manner suggests; the loss of her father in 2019 left a gap in that lineage she notices most when the table is supposed to be full.

## Interests & Hobbies

Running is the closest thing she has to a devotion, though she would resist the word; what she is actually after on the early-morning loops is the particular emptiness of a body doing one repetitive thing while the mind sorts itself out unsupervised. Reading sits in a more complicated place; she moves between business nonfiction she half-treats as professional maintenance and literary fiction she slightly guards, reading more of it during the harder stretches without consciously deciding to. Cooking is a small daily experiment, mostly sheet pan dinners, stir-fry, pasta, and an Instant Pot she has converted to. She rewatches cooking competition shows for the low, undemanding hum of competence on a screen. Underneath all of it runs a quiet, half-spoken wish for some pursuit that is purely hers and answers to nothing.
